## How to Choose a Plan

Choosing a plan comes down to three things: how much traffic you expect, how critical the workload is, and whether you need team or enterprise features. Start with the smallest plan that fits your current needs, then move up as your app grows.

## Plans Overview

| Plan | Best for | Key points |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `Hobby` | Side projects, prototypes, learning | Low-cost starting point for testing ideas and small apps |
| `Pro` | Production apps, growing products | More headroom for traffic, storage, and day-to-day operational use |
| `Enterprise` | Large teams, regulated workloads, custom requirements | Advanced support, higher limits, and enterprise-focused features |

## Decision Guide

- If you are building a side project, exploring TerraScale, or testing an idea, choose **Hobby**.
- If you are running a production app with moderate traffic, choose **Pro**.
- If you need enterprise features, custom agreements, or higher scale requirements, contact us about **Enterprise**.

## What Counts as Usage

Your plan usage is usually shaped by a mix of:

- **Operations**: reads, writes, queries, and other database requests.
- **Storage**: the amount of data you keep in TerraScale.
- **Replication impact**: using more regions or replicas can increase the resources needed to serve your workload.

If your app is write-heavy, stores large datasets, or replicates across many locations, you may outgrow the entry plan sooner.

## Upgrading and Downgrading

You can change plans from your account settings when your needs change. Upgrades typically take effect right away so you can access the new limits without delay. Downgrades usually apply at the next billing cycle, and prorated billing may apply depending on when the change is made.
